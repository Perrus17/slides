Skills, jobs and regional coherence

Measuring skill relatedness and specialization using
information from two million job postings

by Martin Henning, Petrus Garefelt,
Hanna Martin and Zoltán Elekes

Swedish Labour Mobility Lab

The overall aim of the Swedish Labour Mobility Lab is to combine unique contemporary datasets
and historical insights to map the labour mobility structures into expanding jobs in Sweden,
and increase the understanding of the ways in which skills, gender norms and geography
affect who enters expanding jobs, in which ways, and under which working conditions

Background and aim

In recent years, researchers have developed a number of strategies, including a variety of relatedness measures, to approach new questions around regional economic coherence and regional path dependency (Hidalgo et al., 2018).

A stream of studies by now reveal that regions are more likely to branch into related activities, and that activities that are less related to the technological core of regions are more likely to exit the local economy (Boschma 2017)



(1) division of labour is spatial

(2) economic activities concentrate in space

(3) related activities jointly concentrate in a coherent manner,
and this coherence arises because of a regional path-
dependent selection process (Neffke et al., 2011)

While the ideas around skills and jobs have grown into a huge geographical literature, most regional researchers still, for practical reasons, had to rely on skills as an unobservable and aggregate entity.

In its extreme, this turns into a validity issue – when it comes to what kind of qualitative skills are actually captured in the measures, and what this tells us about spatial heterogeneity among skill categories.

Research questions

Which activities are related to each other in the economy, and does this relatedness matter for

    (1) how people switch between working with activities,
    (2) how people are compensated for their strategies when switching and, ultimately, for
    (3) how particular types of activities are concentrated in space?
